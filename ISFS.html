STRANGERS SETS
Encouraging collaboration and creativity in communities
ISFS is a collaborative project deploying an intervention for a space that enables strangers to interact. This project allowed us to influence, engage, and interact with systems through the user’s lens. This 3 week project created a new perspective on public spaces through 3 different iterations.
Duration: 3 weeks
Team: Cierra King, Isabel Anguera, Esther Nam, Vic Yuen
Role: User Research/Testing, Visual Design, Prototyping

RESEARCH

My team and I decided to create an intervention at Columbus Park in Chinatown. We hypothesized that this location would give us the most interesting and un-expecting interactions because of the concentrated ethnic group there.



Target Audience
Elders
⟶ Playing games, performing, exercising
⟶ Majority of population smokes
Children
⟶ Around ages 3-11
⟶ Mostly accompanied by parents
⟶ Usually plays on playground
Insight from Observation
⟶ Comfort
They’re comfortable in their environment and don’t like change because of routine-based activities.
⟶ Community
Relying on community by becoming familiar with their surroundings.
⟶ Homogenous ethnic group
Through several interviews and observations, the elders are predominantly Taishanese, a dialect of Cantonese.



Process

Through 3 iteration, we were able to design and prototype the 3 seperate interventions within the park.
Problem: We noticed that the elders would smoke, then throw the cigarette butts on the ground. It was interesting to see this because the park had approximately 6-10 trashcans located around every curve.

Solution: In response to the debris and litter on the ground, we wanted to create ash trays located at every table. By using recyclable materials, we reconstructed these soda cans into ash trays by cutting strips and folding the metal.
As a team, we synthesized our insights in an affinity map in order to narrow our scope. We identified key themes in our process. This was a form of brainstorming during the earlier stages that proved to be helpful in setting up a good foundation for the entirety of the project.

TAKEAWAYS

⟶ Elder don’t like change.
⟶ Sustainability isn’t prioritized
⟶ Enjoys familiarity in community
⟶ Project was an evident “interruption” to their routine through them knocking it down
⟶ Their interaction gave us a sense of direction for the next iterations

Challenges we encountered were verbally speaking to them or feeling out of place due to cultural norms. My team and I decided that we should try to reach a new target group due to more technical issues. As a result, we transitioned to another part of the park: the playground.

REDESIGN

Behavioral Insight
Simple shapes
Friendly Colors
Mimicking actions
Outcome

The outcome was un-expecting yet exciting. As soon as we set down the boxes, a crowd of young children gathered around and immediately began stacking, throwing, sitting on, and chasing each other with the boxes. The interaction gave us insight into behavioral analysis of kids, specific to this location.

Our key concept for this final iteration was the “exquisite corpse.” We wanted the children to focus on the boxes and treat it as if it had more meaning than the blank cubes before. We created 8 character faces, 8 torsos, 8 lower bodies, as well as 4 hats. We also included orange into our color scheme to allow the top, middle, and bottom concept of three to flow better.

“I don’t think she would have thrown it if she didn’t see the other kids throwing it around”
